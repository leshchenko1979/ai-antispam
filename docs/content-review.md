## 2. Strengths

1. **Authentic voice** — "Я" (I), personal, first-hand experience. Reads like a real developer sharing real battles, not corporate marketing.
2. **Case studies are the best content** — Posts #30 (spam theater) and #24 (porn bots) are genuinely interesting even to non-admins. They tell a story with villains, tactics, and counter-measures. This is the format to double down on.
3. **Transparency** — admitting bugs (#21, #40), explaining limitations (#30: "пока непонятно, как такое блокировать"), being honest about false positives. Builds trust.
4. **Technical depth** — #7 (algorithm explanation) is a comprehensive reference. Anyone evaluating the bot would need to read this.
5. **Good formatting** — clear headers, emoji separators, bold for key points, links to bot/channel throughout.
6. **Cross-referencing** — linking to relevant earlier posts (e.g. #27 → #7, #14 → setup). Good internal SEO.

## 3. Weaknesses

1. **Frequency: too low** — 25 posts in 15 months (~1.7/mo). Channel has 7 subscribers. No consistent schedule. Several multi-month gaps (Feb→Apr 2025, May→Nov 2025, Feb→May 2026).
2. **No English channel** — Missing all international traffic (90% of Telegram audience).
3. **No blog/SEO** — The website has no blog section. A blog would capture search traffic for "telegram anti spam bot" queries.
4. **No cross-posting** — No Dev.to, Medium, or external syndication. The vc.ru article (115 views) was a one-off.
5. **No visual identity** — Photos are raw screenshots, not branded graphics. No consistent thumbnail style. The four photos in posts 31–34 have no captions or explanations — confusing without text context.
6. **No metrics** — No "since launch" stats, no before/after comparisons, no "how many Stars saved" calculators. Product posts describe WHAT but not the IMPACT.
7. **No pricing/ROI content** — The bot uses Stars, but there's no post explaining "how little this costs" or comparing to manual moderation costs.
8. **No social proof** — No testimonials, user quotes, or case studies from real groups. The @BotList submission helps but is invisible to channel subscribers.
9. **No competitor positioning** — Never addresses why ai-antispam is better than free alternatives (@SpamBot, @GroupHelpBot) or paid ones (Combot).
10. **Filler posts (#18, #27)** — #18 is just "Реализовали :)" as a reply. #27 points back to #7. These waste reader attention in the feed.
